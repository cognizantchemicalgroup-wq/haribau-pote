## Naming Strategy

### Why These Names Work:

1. **Brand Integration** - "Haribau's" creates brand ownership
2. **Emotional Connection** - "Aai's Sunday Special" evokes home cooking
3. **Location Pride** - "Panvel Pride" and "Konkan Coast" show regional authenticity
4. **Descriptive & Catchy** - "Teekha Laal" (spicy red), "Desi Tadka" (traditional tempering)
5. **Heritage Appeal** - "Heritage" and "Maharaja" convey premium quality
6. **Function-Based** - "Gharoghari Garghuti" (home-style curry) tells usage

### About "Mirchi/Masala" Labeling:

**RECOMMENDATION:** Yes, include reference contextually!

‚úÖ **Good Examples:**
- "Haribau's Teekha **Laal Mirchi Powder**" - Clear it's chili
- "Traditional **Pote Masala**" - Clear it's a spice blend
- "Desi Tadka **Chai Masala**" - Clear it's for tea

‚úÖ **Benefits:**
1. Helps customers understand what the product is
2. Improves SEO (people search "garam masala" not just "garam")
3. Reduces confusion for new customers
4. Maintains authenticity

üéØ **Current Approach:**
All products already include "Masala" or "Powder" in their names, which is PERFECT! This helps customers immediately understand:
- **Masala** = Spice blend (mixture of spices)
- **Powder** = Single ground spice
